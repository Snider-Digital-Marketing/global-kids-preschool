Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between Reggio and Montessori?

We have a lot in common with Montessori (child led, natural materials, outdoor learning‚Ä¶) Reggio has more freedom with materials. A water pitcher can be a rocket or a car garage or a dinosaur's house. Where Montessori is branded and comes with specific training and materials- Reggio adapts to fit the needs of the local culture and community of learners.

Do you provide meals?

Lunches and snacks are packed from home. We do not have a kitchen on site. This allows families to provide meals that meet their child's dietary needs and preferences.

My child doesn't speak English. Is that ok?

We have a very diverse student and teacher population. Many of our children & staff are bilingual. We think it's great that you are maintaining your native language and welcome the chance to be a first introduction to English for your little one.

What safety measures do you have?

We are a licensed school with the state of TX subject to annual inspections and compliance. Our classrooms all have security cameras that store 30 days of data. Our entrance is secured with a smart-lock system that families can open with Bluetooth on their phone. Our teachers are all FBI fingerprint background screened as well as CPR/1st Aid certified.

Do you charge extra fees?

We don't charge any extra fees. The only thing you will ever pay is tuition. Simple.

What is Reggio Emilia?

Our Educational Philosophy

The Reggio Emilia philosophy originated in Northern Italy. It is an approach to teaching, learning and advocacy for children.

The Reggio Emilia Approach is a play-based educational philosophy that emphasizes the importance of putting children's individual needs, interests, and desires first. It involves teachers, parents and the wider community working together to support children's overall wellbeing and learning.

The approach involves deep, meaningful exploration, inquiry and involvement and uses a variety of tools and methods to support meaningful learning. It focuses on developing and nurturing the natural curiosity in children so that they can be creative, innovative and independent thinkers.

Reggio kids learn to ask questions, experiment, explore and think critically. Most importantly they learn that learning is fun.
